Our Mission

Tie Dye for Autism is a Non-Profit organization that aims to provide help and an education to underpriveleged children with Autism Spectrum Disorder.

All of the profits earned from selling homemade tie-dye shirts are funnelled to schools in India that help those that are affected.

Buy a Shirt or Donate!

How can I contribute?

Anyone can contribute by simply buying a t-shirt, donating any amount, or volunteering their time! Simply click on one of the buttons below and you will be redirected to the according page. You're one step closer to aiding disabled children!

Where does it go?

All donations and profits from sold tshirts will be used to provide supplies/housing to underpriveleged children with Autism in India. Our current goal is to provide their school in India (excessively hot weather year-round) with air-conditioning!

How can I volunteer?

If in the tri-state region, you can volunteer to help make the t-shirts! In order to sign up to volunteer, please fill out the form below and you will be contacted with volunteering locations & times. Volunteer hours/cerftificates are given if needed.

Meet the Executives

Monica, Founder & Web Designer

Monica Muthuraj is a senior at JP Stevens High School in Edison, NJ. After visiting and working with underprivileged children at a number of schools in India every year, she saw that children with Autism struggled without necessary supplies and housing. She founded Tie Dye for Autism in 2015 to provide children with Autism Spectrum Disorder that are underprivileged or orphaned with the proper resources and living conditions.



Sabina, Web Designer

Sabina Radjabova is a hardworking senior citizen at Stuyvesant High School in Manhattan, New York. As someone with community-oriented values and commitment to giving back through philanthropic endeavors, she is eagerly looking forward to making sure TDFA operations run smoothly.





Elizabeth, Web Designer

Elizabeth Tulchinsky is a senior at Leon M Goldstein High School in Brooklyn, New York. She enjoys volunteering during her free time at various charity events and is excited to be part of the TDFA team helping alleviate the adversity faced by underprivileged autistic children in India.
